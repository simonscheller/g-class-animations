Chapter I

Salt.

−20.13° / −67.49° · Bolivia · day 02

By dawn the lake has become a mirror. Ten thousand square kilometres of pressed crystal — and the truck looks like the only object in the world.

The salt is harder than it looks. A G 500 leaves almost no print here; the surface heals itself overnight, every footprint dissolved by morning condensation that the wind freezes back into rind.

We drop the tyres to 1.6 bar and drive straight — there are no landmarks to drive towards, only the curvature of the earth, and a thin orange seam where the Andes pretend to be a coast.

Chapter III

Rojo Oro Verde

Vinicunca · 5,036 m
day 08 · 11:20

A geology lesson written in iron, copper, and sulphur. The thin air bends light at this altitude — everything looks slightly more saturated than it actually is.

Up here the engine drinks 30% more fuel and the body wants twice as much oxygen as it gets. You stop talking. You walk slowly. You photograph carefully.

Chapter IV

Green.

Down the eastern slope of the Andes the air thickens. Mud takes the place of salt. Every leaf is dripping, and the truck — for the first time in ten days — looks small.
